CHAPTER 01

Origins.

Vedganga began in a warehouse in Bengaluru in 2015, with a single suitcase of raisin samples and a stubborn belief that Indian bakeries deserved better raw materials than what the market was quietly settling for.

A decade later, the ambition hasn't changed — only the catalogue has grown. Twelve categories, six state distribution, and a client list that quietly reads like a who's-who of India's bakery and food-manufacturing industry.

CHAPTER 02

Sourcing.

Sourcing is not procurement. It's a slow, deliberate act of visiting mills in Rajkot, orchards in Kandahar and enzyme houses in Denmark — of understanding a supplier's process well enough to defend their material to our customer.

Every lot we accept passes an internal specification tighter than the regulatory floor. Every lot we reject is documented and shared with the supplier — because that's how quality actually improves over time.

CHAPTER 03

Assurance.

FSSAI-registered, HACCP-practicing, ISO-aligned. Every despatch travels with a Certificate of Analysis, a lot number, and a phone number the buyer can actually call.

Because in food manufacturing, an ingredient is only as good as the trail it leaves behind.
